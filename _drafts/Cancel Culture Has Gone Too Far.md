Cancel culture is not new.

People have always judged each other. People have always spread rumors. People have always formed opinions before they knew the whole story.

What is new is the speed.

Now one mistake, one old opinion, one poorly worded sentence, or one rumor can spread around the world before anyone has taken the time to ask what actually happened.

And too often, the punishment is not correction.

It is destruction.

## We Have Forgotten That People Change

People are allowed to grow.

That should not be a controversial idea.

You can believe one thing at twenty and another thing at fifty. You can say something foolish, learn more, live more, suffer more, and come out with a different opinion.

That is not hypocrisy.

That is being alive.

But online culture often does not allow people to change. It treats every past opinion as permanent evidence. If you apologize, it is not enough. If you explain, you are making excuses. If you grow, some people say you are covering up who you really are.

That is a sick way to treat human beings.

No one gets through life without being wrong.

No one.

## Fear Has Made People Silent

This is why so many people are quiet online.

They have jobs. Families. Mortgages. Businesses. Responsibilities.

They cannot afford to be misunderstood by strangers. They cannot afford to have a rumor destroy their income. They cannot afford to be turned into a headline by people who do not know them.

Most of my family stays quiet online.

I understand why.

I am the strange one. I write in public. I share opinions. I say what I believe.

Even when my opinion is simply this:

Society needs to grow up.

## Disagreement Is Not Violence

You are not going to agree with everyone.

You should not expect to.

I do not agree with much of what is going on in the world. But I also do not believe every disagreement needs to become a public execution.

There is a difference between holding someone accountable and trying to ruin their life.

There is a difference between saying, “I disagree with you,” and saying, “You should never work again.”

There is a difference between real harm and hurt feelings.

We have blurred those lines badly.

And social media rewards the blurring.

It rewards outrage. It rewards certainty. It rewards the person who reacts first, not the person who thinks clearly.

## Rumors Are Not Facts

One of the ugliest parts of cancel culture is how often it runs on rumors.

Someone hears something.

Someone repeats it.

Someone adds emotion.

Someone posts it.

Then suddenly a person is being judged by thousands of strangers who have no facts, no context, and no responsibility for the damage they cause.

That is not justice.

That is mob behavior with better technology.

If someone has harmed others, that should be dealt with honestly and seriously.

But destroying people based on whispers, assumptions, edited clips, old posts, or one-sided stories is reckless.

And pretending it is moral does not make it moral.

## We Used to Know How to Live With Difference

There was a time when people could disagree and still remain family, neighbors, coworkers, and friends.

Not perfectly, of course.

People have always argued. People have always been stubborn. People have always believed strange things.

But there was more room to be different.

You could have a political opinion, religious opinion, social opinion, or personal opinion without everyone acting like disagreement meant war.

We need some of that back.

Not because every opinion is good.

Some opinions are foolish. Some are shallow. Some are wrong.

But a mature society should be able to handle disagreement without trying to make people homeless.

## Have Your Opinion. Or Keep It Private.

It is okay to have an opinion.

It is also okay not to tell anyone about it.

That may be the sanest choice for many people.

But it is not okay to destroy someone because you disagree with them. It is not okay to believe every rumor. It is not okay to demand lifelong punishment for every mistake.

People need room to fail.

People need room to apologize.

People need room to change.

And we need to relearn how to be around people we do not agree with.

That does not mean you have to approve of everything.

It does not mean you have to stay close to everyone.

It means you can be calm. You can be polite. You can state your view when needed.

And sometimes, the wisest thing you can do is shut up and let people live.

## A Better Way to Disagree

Enjoy knowing people who see the world differently than you do.

You may learn something.

Or you may not.

But either way, you will become a better human being if you can sit near disagreement without needing to destroy the person across from you.

That is part of growing up.

And we need a lot more grown-ups right now.
